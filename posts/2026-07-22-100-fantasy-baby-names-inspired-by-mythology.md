## Mythological Names Rooted in Light and Cosmos

### Solar Deities and Daylight Archetypes

Names associated with the sun carry an inherent warmth in their phonetic structure. The open vowels in solar nomenclature tend to project brightness, a quality that resonates strongly in onomastic perception studies. These selections pull directly from ancient sky-worship traditions while maintaining a modern fantasy cadence.

1. **Aurelion** (Latin/Greek) – Derived from *aurum* (gold); evokes the gilded light of dawn deities.
2. **Solara** (Roman) – Feminine form of Sol; suggests radiant authority and classical heritage.
3. **Raegan** (Norse/Irish blend) – While historically tied to *Ráðun* (advice), the solar association with *Ra* makes this a dual-layer choice for fantasy lovers.
4. **Helios** (Greek) – The Titan of the sun; bold, four-syllable name with undeniable presence.
5. **Urania** (Greek) – Though primarily a muse of astronomy, its root connects to the heavens and celestial order.
6. **Kiran** (Sanskrit) – Means "ray of light"; widely used across India and gaining traction in Western markets for its simplicity.
7. **Solène** (French/Breton) – A luminous variant of Salome; carries a soft, ethereal glow suitable for delicate phonetic harmony.
8. **Amaru** (Quechua) – Refers to the sun serpent in Andean cosmology; bridges fantasy and indigenous heritage.
9. **Elynor** (Arabic/French variant) – Associated with the sun; offers a spelling twist that feels both regal and otherworldly.
10. **Surya** (Vedic) – The primary sun god in Hinduism; a name of immense spiritual weight and phonetic clarity.
11. **Apollion** (Greek) – An archaic form linked to Apollo; rare and striking for families seeking distinction.
12. **Liora** (Hebrew) – Means "my light"; popular yet timeless, with a melodic flow that appeals across cultures.
13. **Zephyrion** (Greek) – A masculine blend of Zephyrus (west wind) and Helios; invented in spirit but grounded in mythic wind lore.
14. **Cielo** (Italian/Spanish) – Means "sky" or "heaven"; short, crisp, and increasingly common in fantasy-inspired naming circles.

6. **Sinistra** (Latin) – Historically linked to the left hand and night omens; bold, gothic fantasy appeal.
7. **Nyxara** (Greek-inspired) – Derived from Nyx, primordial goddess of night; the "-ara" suffix enhances its melodic fantasy quality.
8. **Hecate** (Greek) – Goddess of crossroads and witchcraft; a powerful, three-syllable name with growing modern adoption.
9. **Selkie** (Scottish) – From the shapeshifting sea creature folklore; whimsical yet rooted in Celtic myth.
10. **Nocturna** (Latin) – Evokes the night; dramatic and cinematic, perfect for a family drawn to dark fantasy aesthetics.
