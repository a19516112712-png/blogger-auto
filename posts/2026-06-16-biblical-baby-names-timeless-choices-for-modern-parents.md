## Common Mistakes When Choosing Biblical Names

1. **Ignoring pronunciation:** Some biblical names like **Zacchaeus** and **Habakkuk** may be difficult for non-native speakers.

2. **Overlooking nickname implications:** A name like **Jonathan** could become **Jon**, **Johnny**, or **Nate** — each with a different feel.

3. **Choosing overly common names:** **Michael**, **David**, and **James** are beautiful but extremely common. Consider less popular alternatives like **Micah** or **Nathan**.

4. **Forgetting cultural context:** Some biblical names carry specific religious significance that may not translate across cultures.

5. **Not checking full name flow:** A name like **Daniel David** or **Sarah Rose** may create unwanted repetition.

6. **Assuming all biblical names are traditional:** Many biblical names like **Eden**, **Faith**, and **Grace** are modern virtue names with biblical roots.
